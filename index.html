An Analysis of Breast Cancer Awareness

35 Years of Breast Cancer Awareness Month

The tradition of Breast Cancer Awareness Month (BCAM) began in 1985 by former First Lady Betty Ford, wife of President Gerald Ford. She had a mastectomy in September 1974 following her breast cancer diagnosis, following her husbandâ€™s swearing in as President on August 9. It was a partnership established between the American Cancer Society and the pharmaceutical division of Imperial Chemical Industries.

The mission of Breast Cancer Awareness Month early on was to ensure that women were educated about breast cancer and its early detection tests. The mammogram was, and still is, seen as an essential tool used in fighting against the disease.

The pink ribbon was first recognized as the visual symbol for BCAM in 1992, when Estee Lauder cosmetics handed out 1.5 million of them. It has been regularly used during BCAM since then.

The American Cancer Society (ACS) states that breast cancer is the second leading cause of cancer death among women, right behind lung cancer. ACS Cancer Facts and Figures 2020 states that African-American females have 40% higher breast cancer death rates than non-Hispanic white females, despite similar incidence rates.

Every October, breast cancer survivors and those with breast cancer are celebrated and encouraged to share their stories. There are also fundraisers for breast cancer research and other related causes during the month. There is also an annual breast cancer walk that occurs in cities around the country during the month.
